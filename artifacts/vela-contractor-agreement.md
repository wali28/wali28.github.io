## 1. Services

The Contractor will provide the services described in **Schedule A** (the "**Services**") on the terms set out in this Agreement. The Contractor is engaged as an independent contractor and not as an employee, agent, or partner of the Studio.

## 2. Term

This Agreement starts on **[START DATE]** and continues until the earlier of (a) completion of the Services described in Schedule A, (b) termination under Section 10, or (c) **[END DATE, optional]**.

## 3. Deliverables + milestones

The Contractor will deliver the outputs described in Schedule A (the "**Deliverables**") according to the milestone schedule in Schedule A. The Studio will review each Deliverable within **5 business days** of submission and provide written acceptance or a specific list of changes. A Deliverable is deemed accepted if the Studio does not respond within 5 business days.

## 4. Fees + expenses

**4.1 Fees.** The Studio will pay the Contractor the amounts set out in Schedule B. Payments are made **within 14 calendar days** of receipt of a valid invoice.

**4.2 Expenses.** Pre-approved expenses reasonably incurred in performing the Services are reimbursable on production of receipts. Any expense above **€250** requires written approval in advance.

**4.3 Currency + method.** Payments in **[EUR / GBP / USD]** via SEPA / bank transfer. The Studio does not cover transaction fees on the Contractor's side.

**4.4 Late payment.** Amounts unpaid after 30 days from invoice date accrue simple interest at **0.5% per month**.

## 5. Intellectual property

**5.1 Pre-existing IP.** Each party retains ownership of its pre-existing intellectual property (the "**Background IP**"). The Contractor grants the Studio a perpetual, non-exclusive, worldwide, royalty-free licence to use the Contractor's Background IP solely to the extent necessary to use the Deliverables.

**5.2 Assignment.** Subject to full payment of Fees, the Contractor assigns to the Studio all right, title, and interest — including all copyright and other intellectual-property rights — in and to the Deliverables and any work product created in the course of performing the Services (the "**Work Product**"). This assignment is effective upon payment and is worldwide, perpetual, and irrevocable.

**5.3 Waiver of moral rights.** The Contractor waives, to the maximum extent permitted by applicable law, all moral rights in the Work Product.

**5.4 Third-party materials.** The Contractor will not incorporate any third-party IP (fonts, photography, plugins, open-source code) into the Work Product without (a) confirming the licence permits the intended use and (b) listing all third-party materials in the final delivery note.

**5.5 Portfolio rights.** After project launch or 90 days from final delivery, whichever is earlier, the Contractor may show the Work Product in their portfolio **unless** the Studio notifies the Contractor in writing of a confidentiality hold. Portfolio mentions must credit "produced for Vela Studio."

## 6. Confidentiality

The Contractor will keep all non-public information learned from the Studio or its clients confidential, and will not disclose it except as required to perform the Services. Confidentiality obligations survive termination for **three (3) years**. Information that (a) is or becomes public through no fault of the Contractor, (b) was already known to the Contractor before disclosure, (c) is independently developed, or (d) is required to be disclosed by law is not confidential.

## 7. Independent contractor

The Contractor is an independent contractor. Nothing in this Agreement creates an employer–employee, partnership, or agency relationship. The Contractor is responsible for all taxes, social contributions, and statutory obligations arising from the Fees. The Contractor is not entitled to employee benefits from the Studio.

## 8. Representations + warranties

The Contractor represents and warrants that:
(a) they have the right and authority to enter into this Agreement and perform the Services;
(b) the Services will be performed with reasonable skill and care and in a professional manner;
(c) the Work Product will be original to the Contractor (or properly licensed third-party material, per Section 5.4);
(d) the Work Product does not and will not, to the best of the Contractor's knowledge, infringe any third party's IP or privacy rights;
(e) they will not include, without the Studio's prior written approval, any generative-AI-produced imagery or copy in the Work Product.

## 9. Indemnification

Each party will indemnify the other against third-party claims arising out of the indemnifying party's material breach of this Agreement. The indemnifying party's total liability under this Section is capped at the total Fees paid or payable under this Agreement.

## 10. Termination

**10.1 For convenience.** Either party may terminate this Agreement on **7 calendar days** written notice. The Studio will pay for Services performed and Deliverables accepted up to the termination date, on a pro-rata basis against the agreed Fees.

**10.2 For cause.** Either party may terminate immediately if the other party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure within 10 business days of written notice.

**10.3 Survival.** Sections 5 (IP), 6 (Confidentiality), 9 (Indemnification), and 11 (Governing law) survive termination.

## 11. Governing law + disputes

This Agreement is governed by the laws of **[JURISDICTION — e.g., England & Wales / France / Italy]**. Any dispute will first be attempted to be resolved through good-faith negotiation for 30 days, then through mediation, and finally through the competent courts of **[CITY]**.

## 12. General

**12.1** This Agreement is the entire agreement between the parties regarding the Services and supersedes any prior proposal, conversation, or Slack thread.
**12.2** No modification is effective unless in writing and signed by both parties.
**12.3** If any provision is held unenforceable, the rest remains in effect.
**12.4** Notices are sent by email to the addresses listed at the top of this Agreement.
**12.5** Neither party may assign this Agreement without the other's written consent, except to a successor in a merger or sale of substantially all assets.

## Usage notes for Vela

1. **Before sending to any freelancer**, remove the disclaimer banner at the top.
2. **Always fill in Schedules A, B, C** — unfilled schedules make the agreement effectively unenforceable.
3. **Route every contract through one email address** (`contracts@velastudio.com`) so historical contracts are searchable.
4. **Have your lawyer review the first use** — Italian, French, UK, and US freelance law differ on employment-vs-contractor tests, IP assignment mechanics, and force-majeure defaults. One lawyer review (€300–800) covers all future uses.
5. **Keep signed PDFs + countersigned scans** in Dropbox or your CRM — never rely on email threads as the only record.
