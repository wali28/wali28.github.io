Fjord

— brand guidelines · v1 · april 2026 —
01 — the premise

A meditation app for people who hate meditation apps.

Fjord is what you open when you want to think, not when you want an instructor. No streaks. No gamification. No sleep-story narrators whispering at you. Just a calm place, a few sounds from the north, and five minutes that actually change your head. We build for the people who left Calm and Headspace — and want something that respects them.

01

Not bossy.

No badges, no "you're on a 7-day streak." The app doesn't grade you. You come when you want.

02

Northern-quiet.

Inspired by the feeling of a cold morning by a fjord — spacious, unhurried, without performance.

03

Trust adults.

Full sessions are silent by default. Voice is an option, never the centerpiece.

02 — voice

Sounds like a friend who reads more than you do.

Warm but restrained. Direct without being brusque. Never performative, never hype. Sentences are short. Commas are used sparingly.

we say
“Sit with it for five minutes. Then decide.”
Quiet invitation. Respects that the user is an adult who makes their own choices.
we don’t say
“Unlock your inner calm with today’s guided session!”
Salesy. Exclamation mark. "Unlock" implies gamification we don’t do. Reads like every other app.
we say
“We kept the screen black because the session asks for that.”
Explains design choices matter-of-factly. Treats the user as someone who cares about craft.
we don’t say
“Crush your mindfulness goals 🎯”
Emoji-salesy, goal-language we reject, "crush" is violent. Exact opposite of the brand.
03 — colour

Five tones, three of them quiet.

Shore (warm cream) is the ground. Ink is the body text. Fjord is the brand. Mist is the pause. Ember and Moss appear once per screen, never together.

04 — typography

Fraunces speaks, Inter works.

Fraunces (display, with italics) carries the brand voice — warm, literary, slightly old-world. Inter carries the work — clean, dense-readable, does not draw attention to itself. Never use Fraunces for body text.

05 — mark

Five peaks and a single sun.

The logo is a stylized horizon-scape — jagged peaks that could be mountains, could be a waveform, could be vital signs. The sun is a single dot that never touches the line. Five peaks, not because of meaning, because it counts well.

06 — motion

Everything exhales.

Transitions should feel like a breath out — never a pop, never a bounce. Default 480ms with cubic-bezier(0.2, 0.6, 0.2, 1). Where an interaction needs delight (session completion), add a single soft scale-up of the Moss ring to 1.04× over 900ms and back. Never more than one piece in motion at a time.
